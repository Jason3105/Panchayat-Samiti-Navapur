Government Schemes

Explore various welfare schemes provided by Navapur Panchayat Samiti to enhance rural development and improve quality of life for all citizens


Home > Schemes

Available Schemes

Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchai Yojana

Irrigation support scheme for farmers to improve water usage efficiency

Eligibility: Small and marginal farmers
Benefits: Subsidy for micro-irrigation

Soil Health Card Scheme

Provides soil health assessment and recommendations for farmers

Eligibility: All farmers
Benefits: Free soil testing and advice

Pradhan Mantri Awaas Yojana - Gramin

Provides financial assistance for affordable housing to economically weaker sections.

Eligibility: BPL families without house
Benefits: Financial assistance to build house

Ramai Awaas Yojana

Provides financial assistance for housing to Scheduled Caste (SC) and Neo-Buddhist families in Maharashtra.

Eligibility: BPL SC and Neo-Buddhist families without a home
Benefits: Monetary Benefits as per area of residence

Shabari Awaas Yojana

Provides financial assistance for housing to Scheduled Tribe (ST) families in Maharashtra.

Eligibility: BPL ST families in Maharashtra who do not own a pucca house.
Benefits: Financial assistance to build house

School Nutrition Scheme

Provides nutritious cooked meals to students in government and government-aided schools to improve education and health.

Eligibility: All students from Grades 1 to 8 in government schools.
Benefits: Free nutritious cooked meals.

Attendance Allowance Scheme

The Attendance Allowance Scheme provides a monthly financial incentive to students maintaining 75% or higher attendance in government schools.

Eligibility: Students with 75% or higher monthly attendance.
Benefits: Monthly allowance (₹100–₹300 depending on class level).

Free Textbook Scheme

Easy access to learning materials without any application process.

Eligibility: All students from Class 1 to 8 in government, semi-government, and private-aided schools.
Benefits: Free textbooks for students, ensuring access to learning materials and reducing financial burden.

Free Uniform Scheme

The Free Uniform Scheme provides free school uniforms to support education and reduce financial burden.

Eligibility: All students from Class 1 to 8 in government, semi-government, and private-aided schools.
Benefits: Free textbooks for students, ensuring access to learning materials and reducing financial burden.

Ayushman Bharat Yojana

Health insurance scheme providing coverage up to ₹5 lakh per family

Eligibility: Low-income families
Benefits: Free healthcare services

Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana

Small savings scheme for girl child education and marriage expenses

Eligibility: Girl child under 10 years
Benefits: High interest rate & tax benefits

MGNREGA

Guarantees 100 days of wage employment to rural households

Eligibility: Adult members of rural families
Benefits: Guaranteed employment & wages

Deen Dayal Upadhyaya Grameen Kaushalya Yojana

Skill development training for rural youth to enhance employment opportunities

Eligibility: Rural youth 15-35 years
Benefits: Free training and placement

PM Kisan Samman Nidhi

Income support scheme providing ₹6,000 per year to farmers

Eligibility: All landholding farmers
Benefits: Direct income support

Application Process

1

Check Eligibility

Review the eligibility criteria for your desired scheme to ensure you qualify for the benefits.

2

Prepare Documents

Gather all required documents such as identity proof, address proof, income certificate, and any scheme-specific requirements.

3

Submit Application

Visit the Navapur Panchayat Samiti office or use the online portal to submit your application form along with all necessary documents.

4

Application Verification

Your application will be verified by the concerned authorities. This may include field visits or additional document checks.

5

Approval & Benefits

Once approved, you will receive notification and the benefits will be provided as per the scheme guidelines.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if I am eligible for a scheme?

Each scheme has specific eligibility criteria based on factors such as income level, age, occupation, landholding, etc. You can check the detailed eligibility criteria on our website under each scheme's description or visit our office for assistance.

Where can I apply for government schemes?

Applications can be submitted online through government portals, at local municipal offices, panchayat offices, Common Service Centers (CSCs), or relevant department offices depending on the scheme.

How can I check the status of my application?

You can track your application status on the official website of the scheme, by visiting the local office where you applied, or through SMS/email updates if the scheme provides them.

How are scheme benefits provided to applicants?

Most financial assistance schemes use Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT), where the amount is credited directly to the beneficiary’s bank account. Other benefits, such as subsidies or services, may be provided through government agencies.

What should I do if my application is rejected?

If your application is rejected, check the reason for rejection in the official response. You can reapply after correcting any mistakes or missing documents. If needed, you can visit the concerned government office for clarification.
