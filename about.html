Our Story

The Journey of Navapur Panchayat Samiti

Established in 1962, the Navapur Panchayat Samiti has dedicated itself to fostering rural development and enhancing the quality of life for residents of Navapur Block. From humble beginnings, we have grown into a vital institution that bridges the gap between government policies and grassroots implementation.

Over the decades, our commitment to inclusive growth has transformed Navapur into a model of rural development. Through collaborative efforts with villagers, community leaders, and government agencies, we have successfully implemented numerous welfare schemes and infrastructure projects that have significantly improved living standards across our jurisdiction.

Working at the Grassroots Level

The Panchayat Samiti operates as an essential tier of the Panchayati Raj system, functioning at the block level to oversee and coordinate development activities across multiple gram panchayats. Our primary focus has been on strengthening local governance, promoting sustainable agricultural practices, improving health and education services, and developing critical infrastructure.

We believe in participatory governance and inclusive development. Through regular gram sabhas and public consultations, we ensure that the voices of all community members, especially marginalized groups, are heard and considered in the decision-making process. This approach has enabled us to address local needs effectively and implement solutions that truly benefit the community.

Our Journey Through Time

1962

Establishment of Navapur Panchayat Samiti as part of Maharashtra's Panchayati Raj system.

1975

Launched first major water conservation and irrigation project benefiting 15 villages.

1985

Implemented comprehensive rural health initiative, establishing primary health centers in remote areas.

1993

Restructured under the 73rd Constitutional Amendment Act, gaining more administrative powers.

2000

Launched digital literacy campaign, introducing computer education in rural schools.

2010

Received state-level recognition for exemplary implementation of MGNREGA and rural housing schemes.

2015

Introduced sustainable agriculture program promoting organic farming and water conservation techniques.

2020

Launched comprehensive digital services platform for easier access to government schemes and services.

Present

Continuing to expand our services with a focus on sustainable development, digital inclusion, and participatory governance.

Our Mission & Vision

Our Mission

To foster inclusive rural development through effective implementation of government schemes, promoting sustainable livelihood opportunities, and strengthening grassroots democratic institutions. We strive to ensure access to quality education, healthcare, and basic infrastructure for all citizens in our jurisdiction, with special focus on marginalized communities.

Our Vision

To transform Navapur into a model block of sustainable rural development, where every citizen enjoys a dignified quality of life with economic prosperity, social equity, and environmental sustainability. We envision a community empowered through participatory governance, technological adoption, and preservation of cultural heritage, serving as an inspiration for rural development across the region.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Panchayat Samiti?

A Panchayat Samiti (also known as Panchayat Samiti) is the middle tier of the three-tier Panchayati Raj system in India. It functions at the block level, situated between the Gram Panchayat (village level) and Zilla Parishad (district level). The Panchayat Samiti serves as an important administrative unit that oversees development works, implements various government schemes, and coordinates between multiple Gram Panchayats within its jurisdiction.

What areas does Navapur Panchayat Samiti cover?

Navapur Panchayat Samiti covers the entire Navapur Block, which consists of 98 villages organized into 42 Gram Panchayats. Our jurisdiction spans an area of approximately 1,246 square kilometers with a population of about 2,40,000 people. The region is primarily rural with agriculture being the main occupation of the residents.
